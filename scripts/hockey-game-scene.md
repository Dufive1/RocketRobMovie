# "The Hockey Game"

## Emotional Climax — Screenplay Format

**Runtime**: ~4 minutes
**Music**: Minimal. Arena sounds. Then silence. Then warmth.

---

### EXT. CITY — LATE AFTERNOON

The sky is turning orange. Golden hour.

INTERCUT BETWEEN:

### INT. MAX'S HOCKEY ARENA — CONTINUOUS

The small community arena is PACKED. Parents in the stands. Banners. Noise. Kids in gear warming up on the ice.

Max sits on the bench. Full gear. Helmet on. Stick across his knees.

He looks at the stands. Scans the crowd.

Claire is there. She waves. Smiles.

Max waves back.

But his eyes keep moving. Searching.

The seat next to Claire is empty.

### EXT. CITY SKYLINE — CONTINUOUS

Storm clouds gather over the city. Unnatural. Electric blue at the edges.

ROCKET ROB flies between buildings. Cape ripped. Mask cracked at the edge. He's been fighting.

Below: the city grid is failing. Traffic lights out. Sirens in the distance.

Rob looks toward the arena. Across the city. Miles away.

He looks at the storm.

He closes his eyes. Opens them.

He turns back toward the storm.

### INT. HOCKEY ARENA — CONTINUOUS

BUZZER. The game starts.

Max takes the ice. He plays hard. Fast. Focused.

But between shifts, on the bench, he looks at that empty seat.

COACH HANK leans down.

                              COACH HANK
                    Head in the game, kid.

Max nods. Goes back out.

### EXT. CITY — CONTINUOUS

Rob SLAMS into a building, thrown by an energy blast. He catches himself. Shakes it off.

The machine is still running. The storm is growing.

Rob looks at his watch. A simple watch. Not a gadget — a dad's watch.

Time is running out. Both kinds.

### INT. HOCKEY ARENA — SECOND PERIOD

Max's team is down by one.

Max is playing well. But something's off. His heart isn't fully in it.

A GOAL is scored by the other team. Max sits on the bench.

Around him: OTHER DADS stand and cheer for their kids. High fives. Shouts of encouragement.

Max watches them.

CLOSE ON: Max's face. Not sad exactly. Something quieter. Understanding.

He looks at the empty seat one more time.

Then, softly, almost to himself:

                              MAX
                    It's okay...

He looks down at his gloves.

                              MAX
                    ...my dad saves bigger things.

HOLD on Max. Tiny. In his oversized gear. On the bench. Alone with that thought.

SILENCE except for the ambient arena sound.

### EXT. CITY — MOMENTS LATER

Rob makes a final desperate move. He doesn't destroy the machine heroically — he SHUTS IT DOWN. Absorbs the power surge. The city goes dark for a moment.

Then: lights return. One block at a time. Like a wave.

Rob falls to his knees on a rooftop. Exhausted. Smoking slightly.

He looks toward the arena.

Gets up.

Starts running.

### INT. HOCKEY ARENA — THIRD PERIOD (FINAL MINUTES)

The game is close. Max is on the ice.

Claire watches. Her hand rests on the empty seat beside her.

The door at the top of the arena opens.

Rob steps in.

No cape. No mask. Just Rob. Wrinkled shirt. Tie loosened. Hair a mess. A bruise on his cheek he'll have to explain later.

He's breathing hard. He clearly ran here.

Claire sees him. Her eyes soften.

Rob slides into the seat. Claire takes his hand. Doesn't say anything.

ON THE ICE: Max glances at the stands during a break in play.

He sees Rob.

CLOSE ON: Max's face. Through the helmet visor.

He doesn't wave. Doesn't shout.

He just... lights up. A small, private smile.

Max taps his stick on the ice. Twice. A signal only they know.

Rob, in the stands, taps his knee. Twice.

BUZZER. Final period.

Max plays like a different kid. The energy is back. He's everywhere on the ice.

The game ends. The details don't matter — whether they win or lose is irrelevant.

### INT. HOCKEY ARENA LOBBY — MOMENTS LATER

Max comes through the locker room door. Still in gear. Helmet off. Hair sweaty and wild.

Rob is there. Standing. Waiting.

                              ROB
                    Did you win?

Max looks at him. Shrugs.

Then crosses the distance and HUGS him.

No dialogue.

Rob wraps his arms around Max. Closes his eyes. Holds on.

Claire watches from a few steps back. Her hand covers her mouth. Eyes bright.

HOLD on this moment. Father and son. The lobby. The sounds of the arena fading.

This is the Pixar moment.

FADE TO WARM GOLD.

---

### Director's Notes

**The Empty Seat**: This is the visual motif of the scene. Every time Max looks at it, the audience feels the weight. Claire's hand resting on it is the detail that breaks hearts.

**"My dad saves bigger things"**: This is the line that destroys parents. Max isn't angry. He isn't resentful. He's proud — and that's worse. A kid who understands too much is more devastating than a kid who's upset.

**The Two Taps**: The stick-tap / knee-tap is a small, invented ritual between Rob and Max. It should appear earlier in the series (established in a casual moment) so that when it appears here, the audience recognizes it and feels the weight.

**No Score Shown**: The game's result is never stated. It doesn't matter. The story isn't about winning. The hug says everything. If we show them winning, it cheapens the emotion. If they lose, it becomes about consolation. Leaving it ambiguous keeps the focus on presence, not outcome.

**Rob's Appearance**: He should look like he's been through a war and then ran a marathon. The bruise, the untucked shirt, the loosened tie — every detail tells the audience what he sacrificed to be here. He didn't fly. He ran.
