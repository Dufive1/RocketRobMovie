# "The Greatest Hero Ever"

## Opening Sequence — Screenplay Format

**Runtime**: ~5 minutes
**Music**: Epic orchestral building to warm acoustic transition

---

FADE IN:

### BLACK SCREEN

We hear breathing. Then a child's voice — hushed, reverent, dramatic.

                              MAX (V.O.)
                    Every city has a hero...

A single point of light appears in the darkness. It grows — warm gold, pulsing.

                              MAX (V.O.)
                    ...but OUR city has the greatest
                    hero EVER.

### EXT. CITY SKYLINE — NIGHT

MUSIC SWELLS. The camera sweeps across a gleaming skyline — rounded Pixar architecture, a signature bridge lit golden against deep navy sky, the Titan Sports Tech tower anchoring the horizon.

Lightning CRACKS across the sky. Electric blue. Violent.

### EXT. ROOFTOP — CONTINUOUS

VICTOR VOLT hovers above the city, arms outstretched. Electricity arcs between his fingers. His tech suit pulses with blue energy. Below him, a massive machine hums — cables running into the city grid.

                              VICTOR VOLT
                    This city deserves better than
                    ordinary.

Cars below lose power. Streetlights flicker and die. One by one, blocks go dark.

### EXT. SKY ABOVE CITY — CONTINUOUS

A RED STREAK cuts across the frame.

ROCKET ROB bursts into view — cape flowing, mask bright, eyes blazing sky blue. He banks hard around a skyscraper, dodging a bolt of lightning that EXPLODES against the building.

                              MAX (V.O.)
                    He's faster than a slapshot...

Rob weaves between buildings, closing on Volt.

                              MAX (V.O.)
                    ...stronger than a Zamboni...

Rob catches a falling chunk of debris and HURLS it aside.

                              MAX (V.O.)
                    ...and he NEVER gives up.

### EXT. ROOFTOP — CONTINUOUS

Rob lands on the roof opposite Volt. They face each other. The wind whips Rob's cape. Rain begins to fall.

                              VICTOR VOLT
                    You could have been great, Rob.

                              ROCKET ROB
                    I'm working on it.

Rob CHARGES. Volt FIRES a massive bolt of electricity. Rob dodges — barely — slides across the wet rooftop, and LEAPS.

EPIC SLOW MOTION: Rob sailing through the air, cape spread, fist forward, rain suspended around him —

He connects. The machine SPARKS. The city grid SURGES back to life. Lights cascade on across the skyline like a wave.

Rob stands on the rooftop. Wind in his cape. City glowing behind him. Heroic.

                              MAX (V.O.)
                    The GREATEST hero...

MUSIC PEAKS.

                              MAX (V.O.)
                    ...EVER.

BEAT.

### SMASH CUT TO:

### INT. CARTER KITCHEN — MORNING

SILENCE.

Then: the sound of water SPRAYING.

ROB CARTER — no mask, no cape, wearing a wrinkled dress shirt with the sleeves rolled up — is on his back under the kitchen sink. Water sprays him in the face.

                              ROB
                    Ow. Ow ow ow.

He fumbles with a wrench. More water.

                              ROB
                    Come ON.

PAN TO: MAX (8), standing in the kitchen doorway wearing a hockey helmet and pajamas. He holds two action figures. His eyes are HUGE.

He leans toward the sink and whispers:

                              MAX
                         (dramatic whisper)
                    Rocket Rob... repairs the
                    Hydro Blaster.

Rob slides out from under the sink. Soaking wet. Hair plastered to his forehead. He looks at Max.

Max grins.

Rob can't help it. He grins back.

                              ROB
                    The Hydro Blaster is... not
                    cooperating.

CLAIRE walks in. Blue blazer. Coffee mug. She surveys the flooded kitchen floor with absolute calm.

                              CLAIRE
                    Did the city survive?

Rob looks at the puddle. Looks at Claire.

                              ROB
                    Barely.

Claire steps over the puddle. Kisses Rob on the forehead. Hands Max a granola bar.

                              CLAIRE
                    My heroes.

Max BEAMS. He turns to his action figures.

                              MAX
                         (whispering to toys)
                    Another successful mission.

Rob watches Max walk away. The grin fades slightly. He looks down at the wrench. At the broken sink. At the clock on the wall — he's already late for work.

He takes a breath. Squares his shoulders. Picks up the wrench.

                              ROB
                         (to himself, quiet)
                    Greatest hero ever.

He doesn't quite believe it.

HOLD on Rob's face. The tiniest crack in the confidence.

TITLE CARD:

# ROCKET ROB

FADE OUT.

---

### Director's Notes

**The Hook**: Kids just watched an incredible superhero battle. They're pumped. Then the hard cut to the kitchen reframes everything — was that real? Was that Max's imagination? The audience doesn't know, and that ambiguity is the engine of the series.

**The Emotional Setup**: Rob's quiet "Greatest hero ever" to himself — with that small crack in his confidence — tells parents everything they need to know about this story. He's trying. He's not sure it's enough. That's the whole movie in one moment.

**Tone Transition**: The shift from epic orchestral to warm, quiet kitchen sounds should feel like waking up from a dream. That's the tonal space Rocket Rob lives in.
