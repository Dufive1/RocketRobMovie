# Claire Lavoie (Vela) — Character Profile

> "I was Vela before you were Rocket Rob."

## Real Name

Claire Lavoie

## Hero Name

Vela (retired before the film begins; revealed mid-film)

## Age

Late 20s to early 30s (same range as Rob)

## Role

The emotional anchor AND the film's second-act twist. Claire isn't "the wife" — she's a former superhero who chose family over the cape, and she's been carrying the weight of that choice alone ever since.

## B-Theme

**"Being strong doesn't mean doing it alone. Letting others in makes you a hero too."**

Claire's arc runs parallel to Rob's. While he learns that showing up IS heroism, she learns that doing everything herself isn't strength — it's isolation.

## The Core Truth

Claire was Vela. She chose to step back when Max was born. She's never told Rob. She's carried the family — and her secret — alone for years, believing that's what strong people do.

## Desire

To be capable in her own right. To prove she can manage everything — the house, Max, Rob's absences, and the secret weight of who she used to be — without needing help.

## Flaw

Reactive perfectionism. She takes too much on herself, which isolates her emotionally. She doesn't complain, she doesn't ask for help, she just... does more. The invisible load grows until it almost breaks her.

## Fear

- Losing control of the family if she lets someone else lead
- Being seen as ordinary when she knows she's anything but
- Rob failing — so she overcompensates, carrying both his weight and hers

## Character Arc

| Beat | Timing | What Happens |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Start | Act One | Calm, competent, subtly too capable. Deadpan humor. The audience sees a great mom. |
| Seeds | Act One | Quick, knowing reactions. Catches things she shouldn't. Moves a little too fast. The audience doesn't notice yet. |
| Rising load | Act Two | Rob's double life means Claire carries more. She covers for him. Drives Max. Handles the house. The invisible load becomes visible. |
| The crack | Mid-Act Two | She's tired. Sharp where she used to be warm. A flash of frustration she immediately buries. |
| Confrontation | Act Two low point | She tells Rob: "You're trying to save everyone except the people in this house." Then: "I know what you're trying to do. I've done it." |
| The Reveal | ~65 min | Volt attacks the Lavoie neighborhood. Claire moves like someone who's done this before. Rob sees it. "Claire... how did you—" She drops the mask: "I was Vela before you were Rocket Rob." |
| The Choice Explained | ~67 min | Rob asks why she stopped. Claire, looking toward Max's room: "Because I chose something harder." |
| Active Hero | Act Three | Claire is Vela again. Not because she wants to be, but because the family needs her to be. She coordinates the ground while Rob takes the sky. |
| Resolution | Finale | She's no longer carrying everything alone. Rob sees her. They stand in the kitchen together. Equal. Present. |

## Personality

| Trait | Expression |
|-------|-----------|
| Confident and proactive | Doesn't wait for problems — she's already solved them |
| Warm but guarded | Kind to everyone, emotionally honest with almost no one |
| Inventive | Practical creativity — jury-rigs solutions, improvises, thinks on her feet |
| Wry humor | Deadpan reactions that are funnier because she's secretly the most capable person in the room |
| Quietly fierce | When the family is threatened, the calm drops and something sharper appears |
| Playful (when she lets herself be) | Nicknames for Max and Rob, silly voices during bedtime, competitive streak at family game night |

## Expression Range

- Warm and amused (watching Rob and Max play)
- Subtly knowing (when Rob describes something "weird" — she recognizes it)
- Carrying invisible weight (the look of someone managing everything while appearing fine)
- The crack (a flash of frustration or exhaustion she buries immediately)
- Fierce clarity (the Vela reveal — the mask drops)
- Relief (the resolution — she doesn't have to carry it alone anymore)

### Vela Suit (Revealed Act Two)

The suit has been in a box in the back of Claire's closet for years. She hasn't worn it since before Max was born. It still fits. But it shouldn't look pristine — there's a scuff on the left boot, a faded patch on the shoulder, a scratch across the visor she never fixed. This tells the audience it's real. It has history. It was *used*.

| Element | Description | Hex | Purpose |
|---------|-------------|-----|---------|
| Base suit | Deep navy body, fitted and streamlined | `#1A2744` | Speed silhouette — no drag, no excess. Contrasts Rob's boxy gray suit. |
| Chest panel | Rocket Red V-shaped accent across the chest | `#E01B1B` | Ties to the family palette. The "V" is for Vela, but also mirrors Rob's RR shape inverted. |
| Boots | Knee-high, deep navy with red speed-line trim | `#1A2744` / `#E01B1B` | Streamlined for movement. Left boot has a visible scuff — old battle damage. |
| Gloves | Fingerless, navy with gold grip pads | `#1A2744` / `#D79B2E` | Practical. She fights with her hands. The gold links to the family palette without copying Rob's gold trim. |
| Visor | Translucent sky blue, half-face | `#3FA9F5` | Protects the eyes during speed runs. Scratch across the left side she never repaired. Pushes up onto her forehead when she's talking — a character gesture. |
| Belt | Thin, functional, warm gold clasp | `#D79B2E` | Utility, not icon. Where Rob's belt buckle is a brand symbol, Claire's is a tool. Holds small devices. |
| Speed-line accents | Red lines along the arms and legs | `#E01B1B` | Visual motion cues — even when standing still, the suit suggests she's about to move. |
| No cape | — | — | She's past that. She's faster without one. The absence of a cape is a design statement: Claire chose efficiency over spectacle. |

**Design contrast with Rocket Rob:**

| | Rocket Rob | Vela |
|--|-----------|----------|
| Silhouette | Broad, caped, corporate hero | Sleek, capeless, runner's frame |
| Movement style | Power-based — strength, absorption, endurance | Speed-based — precision, evasion, momentum |
| Color emphasis | Gray suit with red accents | Navy suit with red and gold accents |
| Design philosophy | Imagination made visible (the cape is a dish towel made epic) | Experience made functional (the suit is a tool, not a costume) |
| Wear | Scratched and dented from tonight's fight | Scuffed and faded from years in storage |

**Animation notes**: Vela moves differently from Rocket Rob. Where Rob plants his feet and absorbs force, Claire is fluid — low center of gravity, weight shifting constantly, always in motion even when standing still. Her idle pose should have a slight lean, like she's about to sprint. When she runs, the speed-line accents on the suit blur. When she stops, there's a half-second delay where the air catches up to her — a small visual signature.

## Body Proportions

- Height ratio: 6.5–7 heads tall
- Pixar-stylized: elegant but warm
- Confident posture — stands tall, shoulders back
- Expressive hands and face
- In motion: noticeably fluid and fast, even in everyday scenes (the seeds)

## The Vela Reveal — Detailed

This is the film's second-act turning point. It recontextualizes everything:

- Her calm in crisis → she's been here before
- Her knowing looks → she wasn't learning, she was remembering
- Her competence → not just a capable mom — a former superhero who chose a harder mission
- Her frustration → the invisible load of a secret identity ON TOP of everything else

The reveal should feel like the audience has been watching two movies. In one, Claire is a great mom. In the other, she's a retired hero who's been hiding in plain sight. Both are true. That's the Pixar moment for adults.

For kids: "WHOA, the mom is a superhero too!"
For parents: "She gave up everything to be here. And nobody noticed."

## Active Role in the Final Battle

Claire doesn't take over Rob's fight. She handles the ground while he handles the sky. They're a team for the first time.

- Coordinates civilian evacuation
- Protects the arena where Max's rescheduled game is happening
- Outsmarts Volt's ground-level tech with experience Volt didn't know she had
- Max sees both parents are heroes — in different ways

## Character Constellation Role

**The Grounding Force — with her own arc.** Claire's belief is: "Great heroes protect what matters most." But her flaw is believing she has to protect it alone. Her arc is learning that partnership — not isolation — is what makes a family strong.

She's the only character in the constellation who has *already answered* the film's central question. She knows heroism means showing up. She chose it years ago. Her journey is about letting someone else see that choice — and share the weight.
