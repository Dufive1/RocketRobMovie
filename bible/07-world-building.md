# Rocket Rob — World Building

## The City

Fully fictional, unnamed — the Pixar way.

No real city, no geography to pin down. Just "the city," the way Metroville and Monstropolis work in their respective films. Specific enough to feel lived-in, universal enough that any kid anywhere recognizes it as *their* city.

The city itself is neutral. The Canadian identity belongs to **Rob** — it's who he is, not where he lives. He brought it with him. It shows up in his home, his humor, his wardrobe, and the sport he passed down to Max. The city could be anywhere. Rob Lavoie is unmistakably Canadian. That distinction is the point.

### Visual Character

The city is a character in its own right. It should feel:

- **Optimistic** — Even at night, even during a Volt attack, the palette stays rich and warm. This is a world kids want to live in.
- **Modern but friendly** — Clean lines softened by rounded Pixar architecture. Think Apple Store meets neighborhood bakery.
- **Lived-in** — Not sterile. There are coffee shops, parks, a worn-in hockey arena. It has texture.

### Key Landmarks

| Location | Visual Identity | Narrative Purpose |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Lavoie Family Home** | Warm two-story house, slightly cluttered yard, hockey net in the driveway. Inside: drawings on the fridge, scattered hockey gear, a cozy kitchen. | The emotional center. Home base. Where the real story happens. |
| **Titan Sports Tech Tower** | Sleek glass tower rising above the skyline. Cool blues and silver. Corporate clean. The biggest building in the city. | Rob's workplace. Source of tech, stress, and Victor Watt's ambition. Visual contrast to the warm Lavoie home. |
| **Max's Hockey Arena** | Small, warm, community-scale rink. Bright ice, colorful banners, wooden bleachers. Smells like cold air and hot chocolate. | Where Rob and Max connect. The championship game here is the emotional climax. |
| **The Signature Bridge** | A distinctive arched bridge connecting two parts of the city. Lit warmly at night. Appears in skyline shots. | Visual anchor for establishing shots. Recognizable in silhouette. |
| **The Park** | Green space with a view of the skyline. Where Rob and Max have played since Max was small. A bench with their names scratched into it. | Quiet emotional scenes. The montage location. |

### Architecture Rules

- Rounded shapes (no harsh angles except in Volt's spaces)
- Soft lighting with warm color temperature
- Bright color accents against neutral bases
- Buildings have personality — leaning, curved, slightly exaggerated
- No photorealism. Everything is stylized.

### Time of Day Palette

| Time | Palette | Mood |
|------|---------|------|
| Morning | Warm golds, soft blues | Optimistic, new day |
| Afternoon | Bright, saturated, full spectrum | Energy, adventure |
| Golden hour | Deep amber, long shadows | Emotional, reflective |
| Night | Rich navy and deep blue (never black) | Dramatic but still warm |
| Storm (Volt scenes) | Electric blue and purple against dark sky | Threat, but still beautiful |

## Titan Sports Tech

Rob's employer. A leading sports technology company.

### What They Do
Titan Sports Tech develops advanced technology for athletic performance — motion capture, training AI, biometric systems, stadium tech. It's a plausible near-future tech company that bridges sports and innovation.

### Visual Design
- Glass and steel exterior
- Clean, minimalist interiors
- Screens and holograms everywhere
- Cool blue-white lighting (contrasts the warm Lavoie home)
- Conference rooms with skyline views

### Narrative Function
Titan is where Rob's work-life tension lives. It's also Victor Watt's origin — he rose through Titan's ranks before breaking away. The technology Rob uses as Rocket Rob may have originated here.

### Key Spaces Within Titan
- **Rob's cubicle** — Messy. Team Canada mug. Family photos. Stacks of paper. A drawing from Max pinned to the wall.
- **The executive floor** — Where Volt used to work. Pristine. Empty. The opposite of Rob's space.
- **The lab** — Where experimental tech is developed. The cat sidekick's harness gadgets were sourced (accidentally) from here.

## Rob's Canadian Identity in the World

Canadian is who Rob *is*, not necessarily where he lives.

### How It Shows

- **At home**: Worn hockey jersey on weekend mornings. Canadian flag magnet on the fridge. Maple syrup in quantities that raise eyebrows.
- **At work**: Team Canada mug. Small maple leaf pin on his suit lapel. Apologizes to the coffee machine.
- **In conversation**: Says "sorry" reflexively. Dry, self-deprecating humor. Means it every time.
- **Through Max**: Max wears a Canadian-themed hockey jersey. Hockey is their shared language — it's the specific piece of who Rob is that Max inherited and made his own.

### What It Means

In this city, hockey may or may not be the main sport. It doesn't matter. The way Rob and Max love it — together, on a community rink, in slightly-too-big gear — is entirely theirs. It's not just "dad and son like sports." It's a piece of Canadian identity passed from father to son, planted in whatever city they happen to be in.

The unnamed city gives the story universal reach. Rob's Canadian identity gives it a specific, warm, human texture. Both can be true at once. The city is anywhere. Rob is from somewhere. That somewhere shaped him — and that shaping is visible in everything he does.

### Victor Watt Contrast

Both Rob and Victor are originally Canadian. Victor erased every trace of where he came from to pursue corporate ambition. Rob held onto his roots — the jersey, the accent, the humor, the apologies. Same origin, opposite choices. The Canadian identity Rob carries quietly (maple leaf pin, hockey jersey at home) is something Victor deliberately abandoned. It makes their conflict personal and thematic, not just physical.

## The Two Worlds

Rocket Rob operates in a blurred space between reality and imagination.

### The Real World
- Warm, grounded color palette
- Soft lighting
- Everyday textures (kitchen, office, arena)
- Physics mostly apply

### Max's Imagination World
- More saturated and dynamic
- Bigger, bolder compositions
- Physics are suggestions
- The mundane becomes epic (sink becomes Hydro Blaster, car becomes rocket)

### The Blur
The film deliberately leaves ambiguity about where reality ends and imagination begins. Does Rocket Rob actually fly? Or does Max just see it that way? The answer is: it doesn't matter. The story works either way. That ambiguity is the magic.

### The Silence
Mid-film, when Max stops narrating, the imagination world disappears. The Two Worlds collapse into one. Scenes that would have been colored by Max's fantasy play out in plain reality. This is a visual and tonal event, not just a story beat — the entire film changes feeling when the imagination track goes silent. See `bible/04-max.md` for details.
