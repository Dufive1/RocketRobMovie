# Rocket Rob — Character Profile

> "A dad who accidentally became a superhero."

## Real Name

Rob Carter

## Age

Late 20s to early 30s

## Role

Protagonist. The reluctant hero. A Canadian dad living in a fictional unnamed city, working at Titan Sports Tech, trying to balance career demands with being the father Max deserves. The city could be anywhere. Rob's Canadian identity is his — he brought it with him, and it shows up in his home, his humor, and his son.

## The Core Tension

Rob worries he's not doing enough for his son. Max believes his dad is already a superhero. Rocket Rob is born from Max's imagination — and Rob's heroism is *powered by* that belief. He doesn't just need to live up to the hero his son sees. He literally draws his courage from it. When Max stops believing mid-film, Rob becomes less brave, less capable, less present. The power source goes offline.

## The Source of His Power

Not Titan tech. Not a mutation. Not a suit.

A child's belief in him. That's what made an ordinary dad do extraordinary things. Every time Max whispered "Rocket Rob saves the day," he was giving his dad the courage to try harder, be braver, show up when he wanted to give up. The cape was a dish towel. The rocket was a car. The Hydro Blaster was a kitchen sink. But the belief was real — and the belief made everything else real enough to matter.

## Personality

| Trait | Expression |
|-------|-----------|
| Capable | Solves problems with dad logic and determination |
| Optimistic | Always believes things will work out, even when exhausted |
| A little goofy | Self-deprecating humor, not afraid to look silly for Max |
| Dependable | Shows up even when he's running on empty |
| Slightly tired but determined | The permanent state of a parent doing their best |

Rob should always visually and narratively feel like: a regular guy who happens to be wearing a cape.

## Expression Range

- Determined (mission mode)
- Tired-but-happy dad (morning coffee, post-work)
- Focused problem solver (fixing things, strategizing)
- Proud hockey dad (watching Max play)
- Overwhelmed but hiding it (the quiet moments)

## Running Gag

Rob constantly solves problems using dad logic.

> Villain threatens city power grid.
> Rob: "Have you tried turning it off and on again?"

## Face

- Big bright blue eyes (`#3FA9F5`)
- Thick expressive eyebrows (critical for emotion)
- Clean stylized hair swoop — the signature
- Rounded jawline
- Confident smile with a hint of exhaustion

## Hair

The signature Rocket Rob hair:
- Stylized swoop with clean Pixar volume
- Slightly exaggerated shape
- Color: Rich warm brown (`#5C3A1E`)

This hair is part of the brand silhouette. It should be recognizable in shadow.

## Canadian Identity

Rob is Canadian. It's who he is, not where he lives.

- Team Canada mug on his desk at Titan Sports
- A worn hockey jersey he wears around the house on weekends
- Small maple leaf pin on his suit lapel
- Says "sorry" reflexively, even to villains
- Dry, understated humor
- Hockey obsession that nobody else in the city quite shares

The maple leaf appears in Rob's personal world — fridge magnets, personal items — but **not** on the superhero costume. The primary emblem is the RR shield.

## Character Constellation Role

**Center of the constellation — powered from below.** Rob sits in the tension between all other characters' beliefs about heroism. He begins believing "maybe real heroes sacrifice their families" and ends understanding "the people who love you are the reason to be heroic." But the deeper truth he discovers is that his power was never his own. It was Max's belief, flowing up through the constellation. When it went silent, he diminished. When it returned as a choice, he was more than he'd ever been.
