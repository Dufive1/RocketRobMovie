# Supporting Cast — Character Profiles

## Coach Hank

> "Kid… hockey and parenting are the same thing."

### Role
Max's hockey coach. Gruff but secretly soft. Becomes Rob's unexpected mentor.

### Personality
- Intimidating exterior, warm interior
- Speaks in sports metaphors that accidentally apply to life
- Respects effort over talent
- Has a soft spot for Max (and by extension, Rob)

### Appearance
- Stocky, broad-shouldered
- Weathered face, kind eyes
- Always in coaching gear — whistle, clipboard, team jacket
- Thick mustache

### Constellation Role
**Tough Love Mentor.** Belief: "Showing up matters more than winning." He reinforces the theme in a grounded, real-world way. No superpowers, no philosophy — just a man who's seen a lot of kids and knows what matters.

### Key Lines

> "Kid… hockey and parenting are the same thing."

> "The best players aren't the ones who score. They're the ones who show up every practice."

## [Name TBD] — The Sidekick Cat

> "..."  *(just stares at you until you figure it out)*

### Name Status
Name is pending. Options in consideration: Patch, Byte, Radar, Sprocket, Spark. The character is fully designed — name to be confirmed.

### Role
An orange tabby who decided Rob was his human long before Rob agreed. Somehow always present during hero moments, always underfoot at the worst time, and weirdly effective at the right moment. Comic relief with accidental truth.

### Personality
- Completely literal and unable to understand metaphor — but in a distinctly feline way
- Does exactly what he wants, which occasionally and accidentally solves the problem
- Deeply loyal in a way he would never admit and Rob doesn't fully realize
- Slowly becomes the emotional mascot of the family alongside the hero world
- The running gag is that he keeps showing up in situations that require him to be there

### Appearance
- Orange tabby, expressive green eyes, slightly battle-worn fur
- Wears a repurposed Titan Sports Tech tech harness — clearly not designed for a cat
- Self-applied RR badge on the harness, slightly crooked
- Small gadget attachments that he probably knocked off Rob's desk
- When he sits in hero pose, he genuinely looks like he belongs there

### Running Gag
The cat takes dad phrases as direct instruction.

> Rob: "Let's wing it."
> *Cat deploys himself off the nearest surface and lands on Volt's head.*

> Rob: "We're in hot water."
> *Cat immediately walks to the kettle and stares at it.*

> Rob: "Break a leg, Max."
> *Cat visibly considers it. Rob makes eye contact. Cat slowly looks away.*

### Constellation Role
**Comic Relief with Loyalty.** The cat doesn't need to *learn* love — he already has it, he just expresses it sideways. He represents the idea that showing up is enough. He shows up. Every time. Without being asked.

## Secondary Villains

### Movie vs. Series Expansion

The following villains are designed for different contexts:

| Villain | Appears In | Role |
|---------|-----------|------|
| **The Shadow Dad** | Movie (Act Two) | Brief psychological beat during Rob's low point |
| **Dr. Overtime** | Movie (imagination track) + Series | Max's imagined reason Dad can't come home; becomes real episodic antagonist |
| **The Countdown** | Movie (imagination track) + Series | Max's imagined reason Dad is stuck at work; becomes real episodic antagonist |
| **Coach Crusher** | Series expansion | Episodic antagonist |
| **The Homework Hydra** | Series expansion | Episodic antagonist |

The Shadow Dad works in the movie because he's internal — a vision, not a physical fight.

**Dr. Overtime and The Countdown** have a dual role: in the film, they exist within Max's imagination overlay — his kid-logic explanation for why Dad isn't home. When Max narrates "Rocket Rob is on a secret mission!", these are the enemies Rob is "fighting." They appear in the colorful, exaggerated imagination track during Acts One and Two. When The Silence falls mid-film and the imagination track goes dark, these villains vanish too — making the loss of Max's world even more devastating. In the companion series, they graduate from imagination to real episodic antagonists.

The remaining villains each represent a different parenting fear and are designed for the episodic format of the companion series (see `bible/11-series-expansion.md`).

### The Countdown

> "Your time is UP."

A comedic but symbolic villain. A corporate figure who literally embodies work overload.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Appearance | Tall, thin, immaculate suit. Hourglasses chained to his wrists. Holds a glowing tablet. |
| Powers | Creates chaos from emails, notifications, and deadlines that keep Rob from getting home |
| Voice | Fast-talking, clipped, relentless |
| Represents | Modern work overload — the thing that steals time from family |
| Tone | Kids laugh. Parents feel personally attacked. |

Art reference: `art/characters/villains/dr-overtime-and-deadline.png` (right figure)

### Dr. Overtime

> "There's always more work to be done."

A time-obsessed villain with clock-based powers.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Appearance | Older, imposing, white-haired. Clock chest piece. Staff with clock mechanisms. Cape. |
| Powers | Time manipulation, slowing/speeding time to trap Rob |
| Represents | The endless grind — the feeling that there's never enough time |
| Relationship to Volt | Could be Victor's mentor or predecessor |

Art reference: `art/characters/villains/dr-overtime-and-deadline.png` (left figure)

### Coach Crusher

A rival youth hockey coach obsessed with winning at all costs.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Appearance | Over-the-top competitive, matching tracksuit, always yelling |
| Powers | None (grounded villain) |
| Represents | The "win at all costs" parenting philosophy |
| Conflict | Max's team vs. Crusher's team. Rob must teach Max that winning isn't everything. |

### The Homework Hydra

Pure comedy villain. A multi-headed monster where each head is a school subject.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Heads | Math, Science, Spelling, History |
| Mechanic | Every time Rob solves a problem, two more appear |
| Represents | The nightly homework battle every parent knows |
| Tone | Kids LOVE this villain — relatable and absurd |

### The Shadow Dad

> "They'll understand when they're older."

The most psychologically complex villain. Shadow Dad is Rob if he chose work over family.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Appearance | Looks like Rob but darker, more polished, emptier |
| Powers | Psychological — exists in Rob's mind, challenges his choices |
| Represents | Rob's deepest fear: becoming a successful but absent father |
| Key Line | "They'll understand when they're older." Rob realizes: *They won't.* |
| Tone | Very Pixar. Think the Bing Bong moment from Inside Out. |

This villain could appear in Rob's mind during a crisis of confidence — an internal battle, not a physical one.
